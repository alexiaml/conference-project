Schedule of Events:
Saturday 8:00 AM to 6:00 PM

Opening Session

TYPE OF EVENT: Talk
WHEN: 8:30 AM to 9:15 AM (Seating starts at 8:00 AM)
WHERE: Regency Ballroom

Chisnell and Quesenbery open up the conference by introducing the concept of accessible and usable design.

Jen Simmons: Tackling CSS Grid

TYPE OF EVENT: Workshop
WHEN: 9:45 AM to 10:45 AM (Seating starts at 9:15 AM)
WHERE: Columbia AB

Learn CSS grid with with Simmons. Step by step fundamentals to make responsive grids for your sites.

Massimo Banzi: What is Arduino?

TYPE OF EVENT: Talk
WHEN: 9:45 AM to 10:45 AM (Seating starts at 9:15 AM)
WHERE: Regency A

What is Arduino and how does it work? Listen to Banzi as he explains how he started Arduino and how he wants to drive designers to continue the open source movement. A Q&A will end the session.

Jeffery Zeldman: What Is Web Design?

TYPE OF EVENT: Workshop
WHEN: 11:15 AM to 12:15 PM (Seating starts at 10:45 AM)
WHERE: Columbia AB

New to design? Want to quick start your career? Listen to what advice Zeldman has to give about the profession in general and at large in the workforce. A Q&A will end the session.

Joe Gebbia & Brian Chesky: Design For Trust

TYPE OF EVENT: Talk
WHEN: 11:15 AM to 12:15 PM (Seating starts at 10:45 AM)
WHERE: Regency A

Gebbia and Chesky talk not only about how they started their Airbnb company, but exactly how design has influenced the creation of their startup and how they continue to to try and improve the design of their company to attract and keep users. A Q&A will end the session.

Ethan Marcottee: Responsive Web Design

TYPE OF EVENT: Workshop
WHEN: 12:45 PM to 1:45 PM (Seating starts at 12:15 PM)
WHERE: Columbia AB

Learn some of the fundamentals of responsive web design that one needs to create fluid design for their sites.

Keynote Speakers:
Chisnell & Quesenbery: Storytelling for User Experience

TYPE OF EVENT: Talk
WHEN: 12:45 PM to 1:45 PM (Seating starts at 12:15 PM)
WHERE: Regency A

Accessibility is a part of the overall plot to a good story. By understanding users and clientel, one can craft a design that works for not just one user but a multitude of user of the world wide web. A Q&A will end the session.

Chappell Ellison: Design For People

TYPE OF EVENT: Talk
WHEN: 2:15 PM to 3:15 PM (Seating starts at 1:45 PM)
WHERE: Regency A

Talk with Ellison about how to make design for people. Hear stories about actual people in order to understand just what accessible design can do. A Q&A will end the session.

Richard Saul Wurman: Design Your Life

TYPE OF EVENT: Talk
WHEN: 3:45 PM to 4:45 PM (Seating starts at 3:15 PM)
WHERE: Regency BCD

Though he founded TEDTalks and continues to give people advice on how they should live their life in imaginiative and non-constricting ways, Wurman talks about how an everyone must take steps in designing their own life rather than relying just on example. A Q&A will end the session.

Closing Session

TYPE OF EVENT: Talk
WHEN: 5:15 PM to 6:00 PM (Seating starts at 4:45 PM)
WHERE: Regency Ballroom

Closing thoughts from some of the speakers and the creators of the conference.
